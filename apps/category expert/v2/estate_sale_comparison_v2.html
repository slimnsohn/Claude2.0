Time honesty

The ideal model is slower upfront, not faster

An experienced operator walks a 2,000 sq ft home in 60–90 minutes today — they're pattern-matching, not cataloging. Photographing every item at 15 seconds per photo on a 3,000-item estate would be 12+ hours of photography alone. That's not the goal.

The ideal flow only does room-level overview photography at the estimate stage. Item-level photography happens later at intake, and only for items that warrant it — high-value flagged pieces that need photos for online listings anyway. Bulk categories get category photos, not individual ones. The SKU-tagging itself takes 3–5 seconds per item with lower-skilled labor — this is the real added time cost, roughly half a day to a day extra on intake.
